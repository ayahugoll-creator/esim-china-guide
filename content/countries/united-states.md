## United States → China: Entry Requirements 2026

This is the page many US travelers land on confused. Here's the honest situation.

### The Short Version

**US citizens still need a visa** for regular travel to China. The 30-day visa-free policy that covers most of Europe, Australia, and East Asia does NOT include the United States.

**But:** you can use the **240-hour (10-day) transit visa-free** program if you're flying through China to another destination.

### Option A: 240-Hour Transit (No Visa Needed)

Fly: **US → China → Third Country** (e.g., US → Shanghai → Tokyo, NOT US → Shanghai → US)

You get 10 days in China, no visa required. This is the easiest way for most American tourists to visit China short-term.

**The trick:** Book your ticket as a multi-city itinerary. US → Beijing (stay 5 days) → Tokyo (onward destination). Use Trip.com to book — their English interface handles these multi-city itineraries well.

### Option B: L Tourist Visa

For direct round trips (US → China → US), longer stays, or if you want to visit without the transit constraint.

- Apply at the Chinese embassy/consulate in the US
- Processing time: 4-7 business days (standard), 2-3 days (express)
- Cost: $140 for US citizens (10-year multiple entry)
- Documents needed: passport, application form, photo, itinerary

### Before You Travel (Either Option)

1. **eSIM:** US phones work in China but with expensive roaming. Get a travel eSIM instead. [Compare China eSIMs →](/posts/best-esim-for-china-travel/)
2. **Alipay:** Link your US credit card before departure. Chase, Citi, Amex all work. [Setup guide →](/posts/alipay-foreigner-setup-guide/)
3. **VPN:** If using a local Chinese SIM, install a VPN before you leave the US. If using an international eSIM (Saily, Holafly), you won't need one.
4. **Departure registration:** Since November 2025, foreigners must fill an online arrival card before entering China. Do this on the NIA app or website.

### Common Mistakes

- ❌ "I'll just do US→Beijing→US on the transit visa" — Not allowed. Onward must be a third country.
- ❌ "Hong Kong counts as a third country" — **It does!** US→Beijing→Hong Kong works for transit visa-free.
- ❌ "I'll figure out eSIM when I land" — eSIM websites are blocked in China. Install before departure.
