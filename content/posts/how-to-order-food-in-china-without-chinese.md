China has arguably the best food delivery ecosystem in the world. Meituan alone handles **over 60 million orders per day**. You can get anything — hot pot, bubble tea, groceries, medicine — delivered to your door in under 30 minutes, often for under $5.

But if you're a foreign traveler who can't read Chinese, ordering food can feel impossible. Here's what actually works, based on real traveler experiences.

## The first thing to know: cash won't help you

Chinese law says vendors must accept cash. In practice? **Almost no one does.** Street vendors, restaurants, even taxi drivers expect you to scan a QR code. Pulling out a ¥100 bill at a noodle shop will earn you a confused stare and possibly a refusal.

This isn't about being unfriendly — it's that small businesses genuinely don't carry change anymore. The cashless transition happened fast, and it's near-total.

**Solution**: Set up Alipay before you arrive. We covered this in our [Alipay setup guide for foreigners](/posts/alipay-foreigner-setup-guide/). Once Alipay works, everything else flows from there.

## Option 1: Alipay mini-apps (easiest for foreigners)

Here's the hack most guides don't mention: **you don't need Meituan at all.**

Alipay's "Mini Programs" section has built-in food delivery that works entirely in-app:

1. Open Alipay
2. Tap the search bar, type "food" or "外卖"
3. You'll see mini-apps for Ele.me and Meituan running **inside Alipay**
4. The interface is still in Chinese, but you pay directly through Alipay — no separate app, no Chinese phone number required

The mini-app version of Ele.me ([饿了么](https://english.shanghai.gov.cn/en-UsefulApps/20240109/89844b5729d64b199463a156bc8ef43d.html)) is particularly foreigner-friendly because payment goes through Alipay's international card system, which you've already verified.

**Pro tip from actual travelers**: Take a screenshot of the menu and use Apple Translate or Google Translate's camera mode. The photos are universal — you can usually tell what you're getting just by looking.

## Option 2: Meituan (if you have a Chinese number)

Meituan ([美团](https://en.hainan.gov.cn/englishsite/APPs/202506/09551f06a3a645bd85e9fd7b0a988010.shtml)) is the king of food delivery in China, but it has a major barrier: registering requires SMS verification from a **Chinese phone number**.

So if you picked up a Chinese SIM at the airport (which we recommend for multiple reasons), Meituan becomes available:

1. Download Meituan (美团) from your app store
2. Register with your Chinese number
3. Tap the **外卖** (waimai / food delivery) tab
4. Browse nearby — many expat-area restaurants include English names
5. Pay through Alipay or WeChat Pay at checkout

A real experience from [travelers on Lemon8](https://www.lemon8-app.com/@mushris/7512436783485960720?region=sg): even without a Chinese number, some users report that Meituan accepts foreign numbers in certain cities. Results vary, so don't rely on this.

## Option 3: Just walk in and scan

For many travelers, the simplest approach is to skip delivery apps entirely and eat at restaurants in person. In 2024 China, the workflow at any casual restaurant is:

1. Sit down
2. Scan the QR code on the table with Alipay or WeChat
3. The menu opens in a mini-program (with photos)
4. Order and pay directly in the app
5. Food arrives at your table

This works at everything from noodle shops to hot pot chains. No Chinese needed beyond pointing at photos and saying "这个" (zhè ge / this one).

| 宫保鸡丁 | gōng bǎo jī dīng | Kung Pao chicken (not too spicy) |

## Bottom line

1. **Set up Alipay first** — it's the key to everything ([our guide here](/posts/alipay-foreigner-setup-guide/))
2. **Use Alipay mini-apps** for food delivery if you don't have a Chinese number
3. **Grab a Chinese SIM** at the airport if you want the full Meituan experience
4. **Don't bring much cash** — ¥200-300 emergency fund is plenty for a 2-week trip
5. **Embrace the chaos** — ordering the wrong thing by accident is a core China travel memory

And if all else fails, walk into any restaurant with photos on the menu and point. It works 100% of the time.
