Every China travel guide says "get a VPN." But in 2026, that advice is incomplete. Here's the real answer.

## The One Thing That Determines Everything

**How you get internet in China.**

| Your Connection | Need VPN? |
|----------------|-----------|
| International travel eSIM (Airalo, Saily, Holafly) | ❌ No |
| International roaming (your home carrier) | ❌ No |
| Local Chinese SIM card (China Mobile/Unicom) | ✅ Yes |
| Hotel / public WiFi | ✅ Yes |

**Why:** International eSIMs and roaming route your data through servers outside China (Hong Kong, Singapore, etc.). The Great Firewall never touches your traffic. Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, Gmail — all work normally.

Local SIM cards and Chinese WiFi route through domestic networks. The Great Firewall blocks everything.

### Install Obfuscated Servers

Most VPNs offer "obfuscated servers" or "stealth mode." Enable this. It disguises VPN traffic as regular HTTPS traffic, making it harder to detect and block.

### Turn Off VPN for Chinese Apps

WeChat Pay and Alipay sometimes flag foreign IPs as suspicious. When paying, turn off your VPN for 30 seconds. It's annoying but necessary.
